## Lesson

To increase the clarity of the broad terms used in *yani sayo*, you can form phrases which begin with a particular word and clarify it further by adding more words before the next particle. These are called descriptors in Adinjo, or adjectives and adverbs in English. In *yani sayo*, descriptors **always** come after the word they describe, and they always take the **o** form – the final vowel in every *yani sayo* descriptor is an “**o**,” and this replaces the last vowel in the dictionary form.

The ordering is similar to most descriptors in Adinjo Journalist or French, and is opposite from how English applies adjectives which usually precede the word they modify (though English adverbs appear to be more flexible). For example:

> **Yani Sayo:** *tin poxon*
> **English:** strong person
> **Journalist:** dyn poraun

Any word can be converted into a descriptor or adjective just by changing the last vowel to an “**o**.” 

> **Yani Sayo:** *memi yo*
> **English:** my parent
> **Journalist:** kuyi (medra/poren)

> **Yani Sayo:** *telwin to*
> **English:** your animal
> **Journalist:** kuci telar

> **Yani Sayo:** *pin xiso*
> **English:** (his/her/their) food
> **Journalist:** (kufi/kuji/kushi) pöén

Likewise, an adjective can be turned back into a noun by turning the final vowel into either “**i**” or “**a**”. When taken as a whole, a noun and its adjectives may be understood as a single thing:

> **Yani Sayo:** *polin to*
> **English:** your strength
> **Journalist:** kuyi poraun

> **Yani Sayo:** *tulit memo*
> **English:** parent’s greatness
> **Journalist:** ku(medra/poren) churito

> [!Note]
> Because a noun phrase can be understood as a whole concept, you can find multi-word constructions that translate out of *yani sayo* as a single word in another language. For example, *tin sayo* is similar to *jan pona* in Toki Pona, and can be used to mean “friend, comrade, ally” in English or “tamano, habijad” in Adinjo Journalist.

You can also add several adjectives at one time.

> **Yani Sayo:** *telwin motlo kewon*
> **English:** cute cub (animal small pretty)
> **Journalist:** mortelar keinau

The order of adjectives can also change the meaning of a phrase!

> **Yani Sayo:** *telwin kewon motlo*
> **English:** cute animal (animal pretty small)
> **Journalist:** telar morkeinau
